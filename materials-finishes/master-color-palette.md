# Master Colour Palette — Whole-House Reference

> One palette source for paint, tile, fabric, wood and accent decisions across the house. Two coordinated palettes are defined: **Public (GF) — Warm Contemporary Indian** and **Private (FF) — Warm Modern Luxe (Japandi-with-luxe-accents)**. They share the same neutral base (warm ivory + greige) and earth-wood vocabulary so the two floors read as one home, not two themes glued together.
>
> Hex codes are the **target colour** — match against an Asian Paints / Berger / Dulux fan deck on site. Always paint a **2ft × 2ft sample** on the actual wall and check it in morning, afternoon and evening light before signing off. Never finalise paint from a 2-inch chip card.

---

## 1. Whole-House Neutral Base (Carry through every room)

These three colours appear everywhere — walls, ceilings, skirting, ceiling planes — and tie the house together.

| Role | Name | Hex | Asian Paints code | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wall base** (all interior walls) | Warm Ivory | `#F2EBDD` | **Asian Paints 7942 "White Sand"** or **0125 "Ivory Touch"** | Every room except feature walls |
| **Ceiling** (all rooms) | Soft Snow | `#F8F4EC` | **Asian Paints 7857 "Snow Storm"** | All ceilings; cove and false-ceiling planes |
| **Trim / skirting / door frames** | Warm White | `#FAF6EC` | **Asian Paints 7860 "Almond Frost"** | Window trims, door frames, skirting boards |

**Rule:** main walls in `#F2EBDD`, ceiling 1 shade lighter in `#F8F4EC`, trim 1 shade lighter again. This subtle gradient keeps ceilings reading as ceilings and walls reading as walls without strong contrast — important for the double-height living area where harsh ceiling shadows would be obvious.

## 4. Lighting Colour Temperature — Whole-House Standard

| Zone | Temperature | Hex (visual approx) | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Foyer screen halo** | 2200K (warm amber) | `#FFB877` | Welcoming, slightly intimate |
| **Foyer + Living + Dining + Bedrooms** | **2700K (warm white)** | `#FFE4B8` | Skin-flattering, relaxing, premium |
| **Pooja niche backlight** | 2200K (warm amber) | `#FFA85C` | Devotional warmth |
| **Kitchen** | 4000K (neutral white) | `#FFF5E0` | Task accuracy without coldness |
| **All bathrooms** | 3000K | `#FFEDC4` | Slightly cooler than bedrooms — better grooming |
| **Study spotlights (BR1 + BR2)** | 3000K | `#FFEDC4` | Focus light, not as cool as kitchen |
| **Outdoor / balcony / staircase lights** | 3000K | `#FFEDC4` | Standard outdoor warm |
| **Camera ColorVu light** | (ColorVu warm) | — | Hikvision built-in spec |

> **Rule:** never mix colour temperatures inside a single visible space. Living + Foyer + Dining all see each other → all 2700K. Kitchen is its own room → can be 4000K. Bathroom is its own room → 3000K is OK because nobody sees living + bathroom in the same gaze.

## 7. Avoid List (Whole House)

These are the colours and materials to NOT use anywhere — they fight the palette or look cheap at scale.

- ❌ Stark cool white (`#FFFFFF`) — looks hospital-clean, kills the warmth
- ❌ Cool blue-grey walls — fights the teak windows
- ❌ Glossy laminate on any visible surface — makes every premium material look cheap
- ❌ High-gloss white tiles — glare in the double-height room is unpleasant
- ❌ Strong contrast veining (black, gold, red) — fights with teak, looks dated in 5 years
- ❌ More than one feature wall in any contiguous open space (living-dining-foyer = ONE space)
- ❌ Three different wood tones in the same room
- ❌ Mixing brass and chrome hardware in the same room
- ❌ Saturated colours (lime, hot pink, royal blue) on anything bigger than a cushion or vase

## 9. Sample Procurement Checklist (do BEFORE bulk buy)

- [ ] Paint sample pots: `#F2EBDD` (Ivory Touch), `#F8F4EC` (Snow Storm), `#A8967E` (taupe accent), `#9CA68D` (sage accent) — paint 2×2ft on actual wall, check 3 times of day
- [ ] Tile samples: 1× full 1200×1200 living tile + 1× 600×1200 bathroom wall + 1× 600×600 floor — see them on actual floor in actual daylight
- [ ] Wood samples: walnut + teak + burnished teak laminates side-by-side — check under both 2700K bulb and natural light
- [ ] Stone slab sample (TV wall greige + BR2 travertine) — bring home and view at all times of day
- [ ] Hardware sample: 1× brushed-gold faucet handle + 1× champagne-brass cabinet handle — check they're the same shade
- [ ] Curtain fabric: 12×12in swatch of sheer + main drape — drape in the actual window for an evening
- [ ] Accent metal: 1× modular switch plate sample in champagne — confirm it doesn't clash with brass hardware in the same room

> No bulk orders before all samples are reviewed. A ₹500 sample saves ₹50,000 in re-do.
