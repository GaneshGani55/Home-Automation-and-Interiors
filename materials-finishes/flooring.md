# Flooring Proposal

Status: Proposed, not yet confirmed.

## Ground Floor Public Areas

| Area | Tile type | Recommended size | Finish | Colour | Grout | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Living | Vitrified, marble/limestone look | 1200mm x 1200mm | Matte / satin | Warm ivory-greige | 1.5mm to 2mm epoxy, colour matched | Best scale for 16'11" x 16'11" double-height room. |
| Dining | Same as living | 1200mm x 1200mm | Matte / satin | Same as living | Same | Continue same flooring for larger visual flow. |
| Foyer | Same as living, unless feature inlay is desired | 1200mm x 1200mm | Matte / satin | Same as living | Same | Keeps foyer, living, and dining unified. |

## Recommended Look

- Warm ivory / greige stone.
- Soft, natural veining only.
- Low visual variation so furniture and teak can stand out.
- R9/R10 slip resistance if available.

## Laying Rules

- Use straight grid layout, aligned with the living TV wall and foyer axis.
- Dry-lay first and avoid thin tile cuts near the TV wall or main entry.
- Keep expansion gap near walls and cover with skirting.
- Use 4 inch matching tile skirting for durability.
- Check floor level carefully; large-format tiles need a skilled installer and proper levelling clips.

## Living Sunken-Floor Detail

- Living room floor is planned as a 5" sunken area.
- Start the drop after a 1ft offset from the surrounding walls.
- Use the same tile for the upper perimeter ledge, step tread, step riser, and sunken floor.
- Add a subtle anti-skid nosing or brass/black edge profile at the step.
- Consider warm low-level LED under the ledge so the step is visible at night.

## Acceptable Upgrade

800mm x 1600mm tiles can look more premium, especially in living and dining. Choose this only if the contractor has large-format experience.

## Avoid

- 600mm x 600mm tiles in the living room.
- High-gloss white tiles.
- Very grey tiles.
- Strong black or gold veining.
- Dark brown flooring, because teak windows and TV woodwork already provide warmth.
