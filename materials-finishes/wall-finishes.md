# Wall Finishes Proposal

Status: Proposed, not yet confirmed.

## Living Area

| Surface | Recommended finish | Colour direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main walls | Premium washable matte emulsion | Warm ivory / soft off-white | Keeps the double-height volume bright. |
| Ceiling | Flat emulsion | Warm white | Avoid glossy ceiling paint. |
| TV feature wall | Microcement, limewash texture, travertine-look slab, or matte stone veneer | Warm greige / limestone | Should run vertically to suit the double height. |
| TV side panels | Fluted teak / walnut veneer or duco-laminate mix | Natural teak / dark walnut | Use as vertical accents, not full brown wall coverage. |
| Curtain/window wall | Same warm ivory wall paint | Warm ivory | Let the teak window and fabric curtain be the feature. |

## Colour Sampling Rule

Do not finalise paint from a small shade card. Paint at least 2ft x 2ft samples on the actual living wall and check them in morning, afternoon, and evening light.

## Recommended Colour Family

- Base: warm ivory, alabaster, or soft shell white.
- Secondary: greige, stone, or light taupe.
- Accent: muted sage, olive, rust, or antique brass.

## Avoid

- Stark hospital white.
- Cool blue-grey walls.
- Dark brown walls.
- Glossy textured paint.
- Too many feature walls in the same open living-dining area.
