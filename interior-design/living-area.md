# Living Area Design Proposal

Status: Proposed, revised after user correction.

## Room Facts Used

- **N wall (with the big teak window): 16'11"**.
- **W wall (TV wall): 11'6"** usable TV-unit length. **No window on the TV-unit portion** of the W wall — it is a fully solid wall available for the feature panel. (The landing window further south on the W wall belongs to the staircase niche, not to the TV wall.)
- Entry: living hall is reached from the foyer at the **NW** side. Step **down** 5" into the sunken seating zone on entry.
- Ideal render camera angle: from the SE side, from the dining hall looking into the living hall. From the TV wall, looking toward the main door, the **NW L-corner window** is the visible feature.
- South edge of living: **staircase niche** (landing flight rising to FF) sits just south of the living area — treat it as a visible feature on the south side, not a closed wall.
- Main vertical feature: double-height void above living area, **16'9" × 11'** on the first floor.
- Ground-floor ceiling: **11ft**. GF lintel height: **8ft** (applies to all GF windows including the corner window).
- North window: centred teak window, **8ft wide × 6ft high**, sill at ~24" AFL (8ft lintel − 6ft window).
- Upper north window composition: centred above the lower window — **3ft wall + 1ft beam + 2ft wall** above the lower window, then a **6ft-tall × 8ft-wide** upper window centred to match.
- NW L-corner window (next to main door): uses the **5'11"** wall on the right side of the main door (outer wall) and a **4'9"** return wall 90° to it toward N. Window height **5ft**, sits under the same 8ft GF lintel.
- Desired curtain direction: double curtain from ceiling/beam level.
- Floor intent: **sunken living floor**. Take a **1ft perimeter ledge** off **all four walls**, then drop the inner floor **5"** (vastu + aesthetic). Step down from the perimeter ledge into the sunken zone at the foyer entry.
- **Volumetric split inside living:** the FF cut-out is **16'9" × 11'**, so only the **northern 11ft** of the ~16'11"-deep living sits under the **~21ft double-height void** (GF 11ft + FF 10ft). The remaining **~5'11" along the south edge** has the normal **11ft ceiling** (FF slab above). A perimeter beam wraps the void edge — the slab/void boundary runs E–W roughly 11ft south of the N wall.

## Design Direction

Use a warm contemporary Indian style: calm, premium, practical, and not too flashy. The teak windows should feel intentional, so the rest of the palette should be warm ivory, greige stone, soft taupe fabric, muted brass/black accents, and indoor greenery.

Avoid a heavy brown room. Teak already brings strong warmth; the walls, floor, curtain, and sofa should stay lighter.

## Colour Palette

| Element | Recommended colour direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Main walls | Warm ivory / soft off-white | Not stark white. Keeps double-height room bright. |
| TV feature wall | Warm greige stone / limewash / microcement | Matte finish; laminate only as a selected accent. |

## Flooring

Primary recommendation: **1200mm x 1200mm vitrified tiles**.

- Finish: matte or satin, not glossy.
- Colour: warm ivory-greige marble / limestone look with very soft veining.
- Grout: 1.5mm to 2mm epoxy grout, matched to tile colour.
- Laying: straight grid, aligned with the west TV wall and north window centreline.
- Continue the same tile into foyer, the 1ft living perimeter ledge, steps, and dining so the ground floor feels connected.
- Skirting: 4 inch matching tile skirting, or minimal flush skirting if budget allows.

Why this size: the living room has a double-height volume, so 600mm tiles will look busy. 1200mm x 1200mm gives fewer joints and a premium visual scale.

Premium alternative: 800mm x 1600mm slabs in the same warm stone look. Use this only if the tile contractor is comfortable with large-format laying and levelling.

Avoid: glossy white marble-look tile, strong grey tile, black tile, or high-contrast veins. These will fight with teak and create glare in the double-height area.

## Sunken Floor

The 5" step-down can look excellent if it is treated deliberately.

- **1ft flat ledge from all four walls** (N, E, S, W) before the drop — the sunken zone is an inset rectangle inside the room.
- Inside the 1ft ledge, the floor drops **5" uniformly** for the entire seating zone.
- Single primary step-down at the **foyer-entry side (NW)** so guests have one obvious threshold; the other three ledges remain passive (no extra step needed unless the user actually walks across them).
- Use the same tile on the step riser and tread.
- Add a subtle edge detail on the entire ledge perimeter: brass inlay, black aluminium L-profile, or warm LED under the ledge nosing.
- Make sure the step is visible at night with low-level lighting; otherwise it becomes a trip risk — especially the foyer-side step where guests arrive.

## Furniture Layout

**Layout (per user direction): L-shape with the long side along the N wall and the return along the E side, plus two accent chairs on the open S side.**

This is a **conversation-first** layout (people facing inward across the coffee table), with the TV on the W wall as a secondary anchor. The long-side seats view the TV at ~70–90° (right-hand turn), while the **return-side corner seats face the TV directly** — that corner becomes the prime TV seat.

| Item | Recommended size | Placement / notes |
|---|---:|---|
| L-shaped sofa, long side | **10ft to 11ft × 36–38in deep** | Along the **N wall** (16'11" total wall, 8ft window centred). Back parallel to N wall, seats facing **S**. Sit fully inside the sunken zone — see clearances below. |
| L-shaped sofa, return side | **6ft to 7ft × 36–38in deep** | Along the **E side**, back to east (soft divider toward dining), seats facing **W** directly at the TV. The corner of the L is at the NE corner of the sunken zone. |
| Accent chairs | 2 chairs, **28–32in wide each** | On the **S** side facing **N**, looking back at the sofa and the N window (and beyond it, the foyer corner). TV is to their right (W). Pulled away from the south ledge so they don't crowd the staircase niche transition. |
| Coffee table | 48in × 28in rectangle, or 42in round | Centred in the sunken zone, between sofa, return, and chairs. 18in clearance from each seat front. |
| Rug | **10ft × 13ft** (sized up — the sunken zone is ~14'11" × 14'11" after the 1ft ledge) | Sits inside the sunken zone; front legs of all seats on rug. Avoid covering the step-down nosing. |
| Side tables | 18–22in diameter / width | One at the open end of the long sofa (W end, near foyer side), one between chairs. |
| Console / ledge | 4–5ft long, 14in deep | Optional on the **S perimeter ledge** as a low decor surface looking toward the staircase niche — keep below 30in so it doesn't block the niche read. |

**Critical clearances driven by the constraints:**

- **N-wall sofa placement (locked):** Pull the long sofa **5in off the N wall**. Use a **standard-height back (32–34in)**. The sunken floor (−5") already brings the sofa back down to ~27" above the perimeter floor, only 3" above the sill — the pull-forward clears any residual conflict. Curtain falls cleanly behind the sofa. Low-back sofa rejected: too casual for a formal double-height room.
- **Curtain stack vs sofa:** N-window curtain track is ~11ft (8ft window + 15–18in stack each side). With a 10–11ft sofa centred under the window, the curtain stack zones overlap the sofa arm zones. Pulling the sofa forward (option a) resolves this — the curtain falls behind, not in front of, the arms.
- **East side is open to dining:** the L-return back acts as the visual divider. Keep the return ≤7ft so circulation past it (E perimeter ledge → dining) stays at least 3ft clear.
- **South side is the staircase-niche transition:** chairs must not block the niche entry. Leave **≥3ft clear** between the chair backs and the south-side ledge edge so people moving toward the stair niche have a clean path.

**Fallback if the L feels heavy after site marking:** a straight **9–10ft sofa** along the N wall (with the same 6–9in pull-forward), plus two lounge chairs on the E side facing W to the TV, plus the same two accent chairs on the S. Drops one big seat, frees the NE corner, easier to move.

## Sofa Set Recommendation

Best option for this room:

- One L-shaped sofa running along the north wall and east side.
- Two movable lounge chairs toward the open south side.
- One large rug.
- One coffee table plus one side table.

Fallback option:

- One 9ft or 10ft straight sofa plus two lounge chairs if the L-shape feels too heavy after site marking.

Fabric:

- Performance linen / chenille in oatmeal, mushroom, or warm grey.
- Seat height: 17in to 18in.
- Seat depth: 22in to 24in.
- Avoid very low lounge sofas because the living room is formal and double-height.

## TV Wall For Double Height

Make the west TV wall the vertical anchor of the room.

Recommended composition:

- Total designed zone: about **11'6"** on the west wall — push this zone **as far north as possible** so the bulk of it sits under the **11ft void** (only the void portion of the W wall can carry a tall feature panel; the southern ~5'11" of the W wall is under the FF slab at 11ft ceiling and cannot continue the same panel height).
- Central feature panel: 7ft6in to 8ft6in wide, full height up to the double-height beam or at least 18ft to 20ft. Centre the panel within the void zone, not within the 16'11" wall midline.
- Finish for central panel: warm greige microcement, travertine-look slab, matte stone veneer, or a premium stone-texture laminate panel.
- Side treatment: vertical fluted teak/walnut panels, 18in to 24in wide on one or both sides.
- Floating console: 10ft to 11ft long x 16in to 18in deep x 16in to 18in high.
- Console top height: about 18in to 20in from floor.
- TV size: 75in ideal for this room; 65in acceptable if budget is controlled.
- TV centre height: 42in to 45in from finished floor.

### Ply And Laminate Option

Yes, ply and laminates are practical for the TV unit if we use them carefully.

- Carcass: 18mm BWP/BWR plywood for console and storage.
- Shutters: laminate on ply, preferably matte / suede / anti-fingerprint.
- Vertical accents: fluted laminate, veneer, or PU-polished veneer.
- Central feature panel: avoid plain laminate if possible; use microcement, stone-look large slab, fluted panel, or a premium matte stone-texture laminate.
- Avoid glossy laminate on the double-height wall; it will reflect light and look less premium.
- Use edge banding or veneer lipping neatly; poor edge finishing is what makes laminate work look cheap.

Electrical/carpentry detail:

- Keep the low TV socket cluster hidden inside the console.
- If the wall is still open, add a 50mm conduit from console level to behind the TV for HDMI and future cables.
- Provide a ventilated cavity for router, set-top box, game console, or amplifier.
- Use push-to-open drawers or slim black/bronze handles.
- Add warm LED back-glow only on the vertical feature edges, not around the entire TV like a gaming setup.

## Curtains

Use ceiling-recessed double curtain tracks. Because the windows are teak, the curtain should be soft and light.

North double-height window:

- Track type: two-channel recessed aluminium track.
- Operation: motorized strongly recommended because of the height.
- Layer 1, glass side: off-white sheer, ripple fold, 2x fullness.
- Layer 2, room side: warm greige / taupe blackout or dim-out, ripple fold, 1.8x fullness.
- Track width for the 8ft window: 10ft6in to 11ft minimum, extending 15in to 18in beyond each side.
- Bottom clearance: 1/2in above finished floor, no puddling.
- Stack side: split centre opening, with stacks on both sides.

NW L-corner window:

- Use ceiling tracks that turn the corner cleanly, or two separate tracks meeting neatly at the corner.
- Keep the fabric lighter here so the foyer-to-living corner does not become visually heavy.

## Ceiling And Lighting

The lighting should have layers, not one bright central light.

| Layer | Specification | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Ambient | 4 x 12W recessed COB, 2700K to 3000K | General warm light in the false-ceiling zone. |
| Cove | 24V warm white LED strip, dimmable | Soft evening glow. |
| TV wall wash | 2 to 3 adjustable GU10/COB wall washers | Adds texture to the west TV feature wall. |
| Chandelier | Vertical cluster pendant in the double-height void | Main sculptural feature. |
| Curtain light | Optional slim linear grazer near north curtain track | Makes the tall curtain/window read beautifully at night. |
| Step light | Low-level LED under sunken-floor ledge | Safety plus depth effect. |
| Floor lamp | 1 warm floor lamp beside lounge chair | Human-scale evening light. |

Chandelier recommendation:

- Use a vertical cluster pendant, not a wide heavy chandelier.
- Finish: antique brass, champagne, black-brass mix, or opal glass.
- Diameter: 24in to 36in.
- **Hang point must sit inside the void footprint** (the northern 11ft of the living, 16'9" wide). Anything south of that boundary is under the FF slab at 11ft and cannot accept a long pendant. Ideal hang point: roughly **centred E–W**, and **~5–6ft south of the N wall** — this places the pendant over the coffee-table area between the long sofa (N) and the accent chairs (S), so it reads centrally from every seat and from the dining-side viewpoint.
- Cluster height (top to bottom of the pendant): about 9ft to 11ft.
- Bottom of lowest pendant: around 8ft6in to 9ft from finished sunken-living-floor level (≈8ft1in to 8ft7in above the perimeter ledge).
- Top of cluster therefore lands ~17–20ft AFL — comfortably below the FF ceiling at ~21ft, with clearance to the perimeter beam.
- Must have conduit + structural hook from FF beam/slab level **before plaster/false ceiling closes**. Hook must be coordinated with the perimeter beam, not the void itself (no slab to fix into in the void interior).

Lighting temperature:

- Living and TV wall: 2700K or 3000K.
- Do not use cool white in the living area.
- Put cove, downlights, chandelier, TV wall lights, and step lights on separate dimmable smart circuits.

## Plants And Decor

Use fewer, larger pieces rather than many small objects.

- One 5ft to 7ft indoor plant near the corner window or north-window edge: areca palm, fiddle leaf fig, rubber plant, or bird of paradise.
- One medium snake plant or ZZ plant near the TV console end.
- One large artwork, if needed, on an available non-TV wall using muted olive/rust/black accents.
- Cushions: 4 to 5 cushions max, mixing plain linen, one texture, one muted accent.

## Automation Ideas

- Motorized sheer and blackout curtains, controlled separately.
- Step/ledge lighting on motion or night scene.
- Scenes:
  - Morning: sheer open, cove off, downlights 40 percent.
  - Movie: blackout closed, cove 20 percent, TV wall glow 15 percent, downlights off, step light 10 percent.
  - Guests: downlights 70 percent, chandelier 60 percent, cove 50 percent.
  - Night: step light 10 percent, cove 10 percent, floor lamp only.
- Add an AC control point if the living AC will be smart later.

2. Exact 5" sunken-floor step line and edge-light/profile detail.
3. Chandelier yes/no and exact hanging point.
4. Curtain motor power point and track recess size.
5. TV wall conduit from console to TV height.
6. Sofa layout marking: L-shape north/east plus two south-side chairs.
7. TV size: 65in or 75in.
