# Floor Plans — Decoded (Authoritative Reference)

**Source of truth:** the **handwritten** sketches (`ground-floor-handwritten.JPG`, `first-floor-handwritten.JPG`) are the latest. This markdown is a faithful text decoding so future agents do not need to re-process the images. AutoCAD screenshots (`ground-floor-autocad.png`, `first-floor-autocad.png`) are kept for reference but are **older and notional** — the handwritten sheets capture as-built changes made during construction progress.

> All dimensions are taken from the handwritten sheets and are in **feet–inches**. Where a dimension still needs a decision it is flagged with **(TBD)**.
>
> **Building orientation (confirmed):** main entrance faces **North**. The handwritten sheets are drawn with S at the top and N at the bottom — i.e. you are looking at the plan from the south side. "North side of the house" = bottom of the drawing.
>
> **Ceiling heights (confirmed):** Ground Floor = **11ft**. First Floor = **10ft**.
>
> **Wall thicknesses (confirmed):** thick double-line walls (structural / external) = **9 inches**. Thin double-line walls (partitions) = **4 inches**. Thin-wall locations: staircase landing wall, utility–store-room partition, bathroom–room partition walls.
>
> **Legend (handwritten sheets):** green hatch = column · blue cross-hatch = beam · black double-line = wall · yellow shading = window · blue arc = door swing.

## Cross-floor relationships
- **Vertical alignment:** GF staircase + FF staircase are stacked on the W side; FF flight continues to terrace.
- **Double-height void:** the GF Living area (16'11" × ~16'11") sits **directly below** the FF cut-out (16'9" × 11'). The void is the visual centrepiece — it dictates lighting, ceiling, and chandelier choices for the Living Area.
- **Beam line on FF:** the blue cross-hatched beam wraps around the cut-out — anything on the FF Living floor near the void edge sits on/over a beam.
