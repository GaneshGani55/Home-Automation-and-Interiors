## 1. Room Read

- Usable room: about 12' x 12'3".
- Ceiling: 11ft, no false ceiling.
- True north: passage / bedroom entry side.
- East wall: exterior solid wall, no window. This is the bed wall.
- South wall: exterior wall with a 2' x 5' teak window at the SE corner.
- West wall: interior side, with a 3.5' x 5' teak window at the NW corner.
- North wall: interior passage wall, with a 3ft door at the NE corner. Door swings inward toward the E wall.

## 5. Clearance Rules

- Primary circulation should be from the door to the bed foot and dressing side.
- Keep W-side dressing depth to 18" maximum so the bed-foot aisle remains generous.
- Keep wardrobe depth to 24" maximum; any deeper will squeeze the bed-side path.
- South-side clearance beside the bed is secondary, so sliding wardrobe shutters are non-negotiable.
- Door swing must clear the bed/headboard zone. Mark the actual 3ft door arc on site before fixing sconces.
- Keep both teak windows free of tall storage, curtain bunching, and AC drainage lines.

## 6. Design Direction

Best direction for this room: warm heritage calm with modern joinery.

- Walls: warm ivory.
- Headboard accent: warm taupe, either paint or a restrained fluted panel.
- Joinery: dark walnut + matte cream, with slim brushed brass hardware.
- Bed: walnut frame with cream linen upholstered headboard.
- Window treatment: cream sheer + taupe blackout drape.
- Lighting: one centre ambient COB, bedside sconces, wardrobe sensor strips, reading-floor lamp.
