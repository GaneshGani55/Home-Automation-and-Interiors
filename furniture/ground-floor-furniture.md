- Walnut shelf colour should match the cavity bezel monitor frame area visually — same warm dark-brown tone.

### Sunken Living Floor

- Keep a 1ft perimeter offset from walls before the 5" drop.
- Use the same tile on step tread and riser.
- Add a subtle brass/black edge profile or low-level LED under the step edge for safety.
- Confirm sofa dimensions only after marking the sunken area on site.

## Living TV Unit

| Part | Recommended size | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Total designed wall zone | About 11'6" wide | On the west wall. |
| Central feature panel | 7ft6in to 8ft6in wide | Extend vertically to beam/top feature height. |
| Floating console | 10ft to 11ft long x 16in to 18in deep x 16in to 18in high | Drawers below, ventilated AV cavity. |
| TV | 75in ideal, 65in acceptable | TV centre around 42in to 45in from finished floor. |
| Side vertical panels | 18in to 24in wide | Fluted teak/walnut, one or both sides. |

### TV Unit Notes

- Keep the console floating to make the room feel lighter.
- Ply and laminate are acceptable: use 18mm BWP/BWR plywood carcass with matte/suede/anti-fingerprint laminate shutters.
- For the double-height feature, combine laminate with microcement, stone-look slab, veneer, or fluted panels so it does not look flat.
- Avoid high-gloss laminate and heavy full-brown panelling.
- Add hidden cable conduit from console to TV if wall chasing is still possible.
- Provide ventilation holes for electronics.

Room facts used: master bedroom is about 12ft x 12ft3in usable, 11ft high, with no false ceiling. The locked correction places the L-shaped wardrobe on the S wall plus a short W-wall tail, the bed on the solid E wall, and the door on the N wall at the NE corner.

### Layout Rules

- Keep wardrobe shutters sliding; hinged shutters will fight the bed-side clearance.
- Keep the W-wall dressing unit shallow so the bed-foot aisle stays comfortable.
- Do not upgrade to a king bed unless the wardrobe layout changes.
- Mark the 3ft door arc on site before fixing the bed centreline, sconces, and bedside sockets.
- Keep both teak windows visually open: no tall cabinet in front of window reveals, curtain stacks, or shutter movement.
